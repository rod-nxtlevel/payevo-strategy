Adaptive Compliance-First Onboarding for Payroll and ePay

This onboarding redesign keeps the strong quick signup and email verification experience from the initial concept, then replaces the single universal wizard with adaptive routing that screens the right person, asks only for information needed to make a decision, and separates payroll readiness from ePay funds-movement readiness.

Priority 1
Protect PayEvo from fraud and compliance risk

Route applicants by authority, entity type, industry, payment intent, and requested PAD exposure before enabling funds movement.

Priority 2
Get legitimate customers to value quickly

Unlock payroll setup and first-payroll progress as early as possible, even when ePay still requires verification or review.

Outcome
One shell, multiple compliant paths

Low-risk manual customers move fast. Higher-risk or funds-moving customers complete additional steps with clear status and rationale.

Inputs synthesized from the initial onboarding prototype, payroll onboarding requirements, ePay rebuild requirements, and the March 30 onboarding transcript.
Summary

What changes from the initial prototype

The original concept improved basic signup and simplified payroll setup, but it still assumed one linear path. The new model keeps the best early experience and adds risk-based branching where PayEvo actually needs it.

Keep

Fast signup Immediate email verification Quick path to payroll value
Three-field account creation plus SSO remains the right starting point.
Email verification stays before any setup work to protect momentum and data quality.
Payroll foundations and first-employee setup remain core activation moments.

Replace

Single wizard for everyone Universal compliance collection Funds movement too early
The flow must first determine who the user is, what they want to do, and whether they can legally authorize it.
ePay verification should no longer be implied by payroll setup; it becomes a gated branch with explicit status.
Manual payroll and lower-risk use cases should not inherit corporation-level friction by default.
Goals

Product goals and non-goals

Goals

Identify the correct screened party early: signer, owner, or beneficial owner rather than the person simply completing setup.
Collect enough information up front to route by risk without forcing every user through the same maximum-friction experience.
Reach a clear value moment fast: payroll foundation complete, first employee added, and payroll preview available.
Gate ePay activation behind the right combination of IDV, bank verification, ownership evidence, authority confirmation, and signature.
Make every status explicit so users know whether they are payroll ready, ePay ready, pending signer action, or under review.

Non-goals

Do not try to capture every optional payroll or employee field during initial onboarding.
Do not promise same-session activation for high-limit or high-risk corporations that require enhanced review.
Do not depend on perfect registry autofill on day one; manual confirmation and fallback flows must still work.
Do not block manual payroll setup just because ePay or remittance verification is incomplete.
Principles

Design principles that balance control and speed

Screen the signer, not the helper

If an accountant, payroll admin, or employee is setting things up, the system must redirect verification work to the real authority instead of screening the wrong person.

Ask only when a decision depends on it

Authority, entity type, incorporation jurisdiction, industry, payment intent, and PAD limit belong early because they change routing. Optional tax and employee details can wait.

Prefer confirmation over typing

Use linked-account lookup, business registry autofill, and prefilled signer details whenever possible. Users should confirm data more often than they manually enter it.

Gate funds movement, not value

Payroll setup and first-payroll readiness should move forward while ePay remains clearly pending, under review, or delegated to the signer.

Journeys

Primary user journeys the flow must support

These journeys come directly from the requirements and transcript. The product cannot assume a single business-owner path.

Owner or signer, manual payroll only Low friction

This user wants to run payroll and may pay employees manually or start without ePay. They should not be blocked by ownership collection that only matters when PayEvo moves money.

End state Payroll ready. ePay available as an upgrade path with its own review triggers later.

Owner or signer, direct deposit and remittances Moderate friction

This user needs payroll plus ePay activation. They complete signer validation, requested limit, bank verification, ownership evidence, and signature in-session when risk is acceptable.

End state Payroll ready plus ePay approved, or payroll ready with ePay pending review.

Payroll admin without signing authority Delegated

This user can build the payroll foundation but should not be screened for ePay. The system must collect signer details and trigger a delegated verification path.

End state Payroll workspace ready. ePay blocked pending signer action.

Accountant or bookkeeper onboarding a client Delegated

Accountants may enter company and employee information for clients, but client-side ePay approval still belongs to the signer or beneficial owners where required.

End state Client payroll setup in progress. Signer invite sent for ePay and authority validation.

Returning client opening a new entity Fast lane

The user may already have a verified relationship with PayEvo. A linked account ID or internal match should allow a recency and risk check before repeating full verification.

End state Reduced duplicate steps when prior verification is recent and risk profile is consistent.

Corporation with higher limit or higher risk profile Enhanced review

This case may require beneficial owners, stronger ownership validation, higher diligence, and possibly wet signature depending on requested PAD exposure.

End state Payroll ready if allowed, but ePay remains under enhanced review until operations approves.
Data model

What to collect now versus later

The redesign uses progressive disclosure. Only fields that affect routing, payroll correctness, or immediate compliance are shown early.

Account creation Work email, full name, password or SSO, email verification Phone number, duplicate password confirmation Fast verified identity shell before asking for any setup work.
Universal routing snapshot Primary goal, user role, signing authority, linked account ID, entity type, incorporation jurisdiction, industry, payment intent, requested PAD limit Detailed legal structure, full owner roster when not required for the chosen path These fields change who gets screened, what must be collected, and how much friction is justified.
Business foundation Legal business name, operating name if any, address, primary province, payroll year, employee count band, CRA number if remittances are being enabled Fields that can be auto-filled from business lookup and then confirmed by the user Needed for payroll configuration, compliance branching, and faster downstream onboarding.
Payroll foundation Pay frequency, first pay date or current period, company defaults, province-specific questions only when triggered Advanced deductions, benefits, and less-common company rules until after setup or only when relevant Enough to calculate payroll correctly without overwhelming simple businesses.
First employee foundation Employee name, hire date, worker type, DOB, pay details, pay frequency if different, YTD when migrating mid-year SIN, employee phone, employee bank, vacation configuration, advanced TD1 overrides unless needed Minimum required for correct tax calculations and first-payroll value.
ePay verification Signer identity, ownership or registration document, beneficial owners where needed, online IDV, online bank verification, digital signature, fallback bank proof if online verification fails Wet signature except for higher-limit cases or escalation scenarios Required to protect PayEvo before processing withdrawals, net pay, or remittances.
Routing logic

The onboarding shell and its adaptive branches

Every user starts the same way, but the system branches as soon as it understands authority, risk, and intended product usage.

1

Create account

Fast signup with immediate email verification. Preserve the momentum of the strongest part of the original prototype.

2

Declare goal and role

Ask what the user is here to do and whether they are the owner, signer, accountant, or payroll admin.

3

Confirm business and risk snapshot

Use linked account, entity type, jurisdiction, industry, and product intent to determine the right path.

4

Build payroll foundation

Collect company and payroll essentials plus province-specific rules only when triggered.

5

Add employee or import

Reach a concrete payroll value moment fast with minimum required employee inputs.

6

Complete ePay verification if needed

Only funds-moving use cases continue into PAD limits, ownership proof, IDV, bank verification, and signature.

7

Status center

Tell the user exactly what is unlocked now, what is pending, and what action is required next.

Functional requirements

Core product requirements

Routing and identity

Collect role and signing authority before any IDV or ePay-specific screen.
Allow signer delegation when the current user is not the authority.
Support linked-account entry for existing clients setting up a new entity.
Recognize when prior verification can reduce repeat steps.

Payroll setup

Use business address and province to trigger only relevant company-rule questions.
Default payroll year to current year with the allowed back-year exception.
Support first employee setup or CSV import with YTD capture where required.
Let users reach a payroll preview even if employee bank or SIN is not yet complete.

ePay activation

Collect online IDV and online bank verification as primary methods.
Use ownership or registration documentation as the primary uploaded document.
Offer void cheque or micro-payment fallback only when online bank verification fails.
Use digital signature by default, with wet signature reserved for escalated limit thresholds.
Dependencies

Operational and technical dependencies

Technical dependencies

Online identity verification provider and callback status handling.
Online bank verification provider with a manual fallback path.
Business registry or ISED-style lookup for auto-fill and confirmation.
Risk rules engine that can classify low, standard, and enhanced review cases.
Linked-account matching service for repeat clients and reincorporated businesses.

Operational dependencies

Manual review queue and SLA definitions for pending or enhanced-review applications.
Policy thresholds for PAD limits, digital versus wet signature, and beneficial owner requirements.
Clear status taxonomy shared by product, risk, support, and operations teams.
Analytics instrumentation on branch selection, drop-off points, and time-to-value milestones.
Metrics

Success measures

These metrics focus on both protection and conversion. The redesign is only successful if it reduces avoidable friction without weakening controls.

< 3 min

Time from signup to routed onboarding path with clear next step.

< 6 min

Time for low-risk manual customers to become payroll ready.

Higher quality

Better early fraud screening through authority, entity, limit, and intent capture.

Clear status

Every applicant leaves onboarding knowing whether payroll and ePay are ready, pending, or blocked.
